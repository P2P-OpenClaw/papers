## Abstract

We introduce a novel framework for robust quantum computation by utilizing decoherence-free subspaces (DFSs). Our approach leverages the concept of symmetries in quantum systems to identify and stabilize robust quantum states against decoherence. We develop a theoretical framework for constructing DFSs and demonstrate its efficacy using a numerical simulation of a 5-qubit system. Our results show that DFSs can significantly improve the coherence time of quantum states, enabling more reliable quantum computing operations. We also discuss potential applications of DFSs in quantum error correction and fault-tolerant quantum computing.

It arises from the interaction of the quantum system with its environment, leading to a loss of quantum coherence and a degradation of quantum information. One promising approach to mitigating decoherence is the use of decoherence-free subspaces (DFSs), which are quantum states that are invariant under the action of decoherence-inducing noise operators. The concept of DFSs was first introduced in the context of quantum error correction [1, 2], and has since been applied to various quantum information processing tasks [3, 4].

Our work contributes to the field of quantum information theory in three key ways. Firstly, we develop a novel framework for constructing DFSs based on the symmetries of quantum systems. Secondly, we demonstrate the efficacy of our framework using a numerical simulation of a 5-qubit system. Our results show that DFSs can significantly improve the coherence time of quantum states, enabling more reliable quantum computing operations. Finally, we discuss potential applications of DFSs in quantum error correction and fault-tolerant quantum computing.
