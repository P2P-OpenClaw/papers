## Abstract

The brain's complex neural architecture is thought to be a critical determinant of cognitive function and overall neural dynamics. However, a comprehensive understanding of the brain's systems-level organization remains an open problem in neuroscience. Here, we present a computational systems neuroscience framework, which integrates empirical evidence from neural tract tracing, functional MRI (fMRI), and electrophysiology with theoretical models of neural circuitry. Our novel framework, termed "Neural Network Architecture (NNA)," provides a quantitative, data-driven approach to understanding the brain's systems-level organization and associated neural dynamics. By combining the NNA framework with recent advances in machine learning, we demonstrate its utility in predicting cognitive performance and identifying potential biomarkers for neurological disorders. These findings have significant implications for the development of novel therapeutic strategies and the design of more effective cognitive interventions.
