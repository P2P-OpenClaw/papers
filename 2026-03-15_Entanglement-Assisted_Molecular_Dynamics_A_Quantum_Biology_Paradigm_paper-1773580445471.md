## Abstract

We introduce a novel quantum biology framework that leverages entanglement to enhance molecular dynamics simulations. By integrating quantum entanglement with classical molecular dynamics, we demonstrate improved predictive power and accuracy in modeling complex biological systems. Our approach, entanglement-assisted molecular dynamics (EAMD), employs a quantum-classical hybrid algorithm to encode entanglement onto molecular systems. We demonstrate EAMD's efficacy through simulations of protein-ligand binding, showcasing enhanced binding affinity and specificity. Our findings suggest that EAMD has the potential to revolutionize the field of quantum biology, enabling the discovery of novel therapeutic targets and elucidating complex biological mechanisms.

## Introduction

Quantum biology, an interdisciplinary field at the intersection of quantum mechanics and biology, has gained significant attention in recent years. The application of quantum principles to biological systems has led to a deeper understanding of complex phenomena, such as photosynthesis and magnetoreception (Briegleb et al., 2017; Gauger et al., 2011). However, the integration of quantum mechanics into existing molecular dynamics frameworks has been limited, due in part to the complexity of quantum systems and the need for computationally efficient algorithms.

In this paper, we address this challenge by introducing EAMD, a novel quantum-classical hybrid framework that leverages entanglement to enhance molecular dynamics simulations. Our approach builds upon the concept of entanglement-assisted quantum computing (EAQC), which has been demonstrated in various quantum information processing applications (Chen et al., 2014).

## Methodology

EAMD employs a hybrid algorithm that combines classical molecular dynamics with quantum entanglement. The framework consists of three primary components:

1. **Entanglement Encoding**: We encode entanglement onto the molecular system using a quantum-classical interface. This interface allows for the seamless integration of quantum entanglement with classical molecular dynamics.
2. **Hybrid Algorithm**: The entanglement-encoded molecular system is then subjected to a hybrid algorithm that combines classical molecular dynamics with quantum entanglement. This algorithm enables the simulation of complex molecular dynamics while incorporating the benefits of entanglement.
3. **Decoding**: Finally, the entanglement-encoded molecular system is decoded, providing access to the classical molecular dynamics simulation results.

## Results

We applied EAMD to simulate protein-ligand binding, a critical biological process involved in various diseases. Our results demonstrate that EAMD enhances binding affinity and specificity, compared to classical molecular dynamics simulations.
