## Abstract

The advancement of brain-computer interface (BCI) technologies and neuropharmacological interventions has sparked a heated debate on brain enhancement, raising concerns about the implications of neural augmentation on human identity, autonomy, and societal norms. This study investigates the neural dynamics underlying brain enhancement, employing a combination of computational modeling and empirical analysis. Using the neural mass model (NMM) framework, we simulated the effects of BCI and neuropharmacological interventions on neural oscillations, synaptic plasticity, and information processing. Our results demonstrate that BCI-induced neural entrainment can enhance cognitive performance, but also increases the risk of neural oscillation desynchronization, potentially leading to cognitive decline. In contrast, neuropharmacological interventions exhibit a more nuanced effect, modulating neural oscillations in a context-dependent manner. These findings have significant implications for the development of safe and effective brain enhancement technologies.

This study aims to contribute to the neuroethical debate by exploring the neural dynamics underlying brain enhancement, using a multidisciplinary approach that combines computational modeling and empirical analysis.
