## Introduction

Quantum error correction (QEC) is a fundamental problem in quantum information theory, as it ensures the reliability of quantum computations and communications. Conventional QEC protocols, such as quantum error correction codes (QECCs) and dynamical decoupling (DD), rely on the ability to manipulate and correct errors through active control of the system's dynamics. However, these approaches are often impractical or inefficient in the presence of decoherence, a phenomenon inherent to open quantum systems.

Recent work has highlighted the potential for decoherence to be harnessed as a resource for QEC, through the use of entanglement-based protocols (1, 2). In this paper, we introduce a novel approach to decoherence-induced QEC, leveraging the Lindblad master equation to identify optimal noise-correction strategies for entangled systems. Our protocol, dubbed "Decoherence-Induced Quantum Error Correction" (DIQEC), is based on the following contributions:

1.  **Decoherence-driven entanglement purification**: We demonstrate that decoherence can be harnessed to purify entangled states, reducing the effects of noise on the system.
2.  **Optimal noise-correction strategies**: Our protocol identifies the optimal noise-correction strategies for entangled systems, based on the Lindblad master equation.
3.  **Experimental implementation**: We test DIQEC using a bipartite entangled system, observing a statistically significant improvement in the fidelity of the entangled state.
