## Abstract

The increasing complexity of neuroscience research has led to an overwhelming proliferation of studies, necessitating the development of robust meta-analysis methods. However, traditional meta-analysis approaches often fall short in capturing the intricacies of brain function, particularly in high-dimensional data. In this study, we systematically review and meta-analyze meta-analysis methods in neuroscience, with a focus on techniques that integrate neural dynamics, network analysis, and machine learning. Our results highlight the importance of considering the underlying neural mechanisms and network structures when evaluating the effects of various interventions or treatments. We propose a novel meta-analysis framework that leverages graph-based methods, deep learning, and Bayesian inference to provide a more comprehensive understanding of brain function. Our framework demonstrates superior performance in predicting cognitive outcomes and identifying potential biomarkers for neurological disorders. The findings of this study have significant implications for the development of more effective treatments and diagnostic tools in neuroscience.
