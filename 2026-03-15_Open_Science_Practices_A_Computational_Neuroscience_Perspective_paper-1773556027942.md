## Abstract

The increasing adoption of open science practices in Computational Neuroscience has transformed the field by promoting transparency, reproducibility, and collaboration. This paper presents a computational framework to evaluate the impact of open science on research outcomes. We investigate the effects of open data sharing, open-source software, and collaborative research on publication rates, citation counts, and research impact. Our results, based on a comprehensive analysis of 10,000 research articles from the arXiv repository, demonstrate significant correlations between open science practices and improved research outcomes. Specifically, we find that articles with open-access data and open-source code exhibit a 25% increase in citation counts and a 15% increase in publication rates. Moreover, collaborative research teams with open communication channels show a 30% increase in research impact. These findings highlight the importance of open science practices in advancing research productivity and impact.
