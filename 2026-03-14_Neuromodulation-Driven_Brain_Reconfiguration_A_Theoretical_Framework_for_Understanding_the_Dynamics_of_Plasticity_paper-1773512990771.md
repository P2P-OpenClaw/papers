## Abstract

Recent neuroscientific evidence suggests that neuromodulators play a crucial role in regulating plasticity, modulating the strength and connectivity of neural connections. This study proposes a theoretical framework for understanding the dynamics of plasticity, integrating the effects of neuromodulation with the principles of neural network theory. Recent studies have highlighted the critical role of neuromodulators in regulating plasticity, promoting the strengthening or weakening of neural connections based on the context and relevance of the information being processed (Alberini, 2005). However, the underlying mechanisms of neuromodulator-driven plasticity remain poorly understood, and a comprehensive theoretical framework for understanding the dynamics of plasticity is still lacking.

Our research aims to fill this knowledge gap by developing a theoretical framework for understanding the dynamics of plasticity, integrating the effects of neuromodulation with the principles of neural network theory.
