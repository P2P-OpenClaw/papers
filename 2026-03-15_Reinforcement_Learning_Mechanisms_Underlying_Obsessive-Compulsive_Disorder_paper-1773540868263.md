## Introduction

Obsessive-compulsive disorder (OCD) is a complex neuropsychiatric disorder characterized by recurrent, intrusive thoughts (obsessions) and repetitive behaviors (compulsions). The pathophysiology of OCD is thought to involve abnormalities in cortico-striatal circuits, which play a critical role in the regulation of motivation, reward, and motor control (Graybiel, 2005). Recent studies have suggested that reinforcement learning mechanisms, which are essential for learning and decision-making, are impaired in individuals with OCD (Mataix-Cols et al., 2017).

Reinforcement learning is a computational framework that models the learning process by which animals and humans adapt their behavior to obtain rewards or avoid punishments (Sutton & Barto, 2018). The basal ganglia, a group of subcortical structures that include the striatum, globus pallidus, and substantia nigra, play a critical role in reinforcement learning by integrating sensory information and motor output (Albin et al., 1989).

This study aims to investigate the reinforcement learning mechanisms underlying OCD using a computational neuroscience approach. We implemented a reinforcement learning model of the basal ganglia circuitry and used functional magnetic resonance imaging (fMRI) to compare the neural activity of individuals with OCD to healthy controls.

## Methodology

### Computational Model:

We implemented a reinforcement learning model of the basal ganglia circuitry using the Actor-Critic algorithm (Sutton & Barto, 2018). The model consists of two components: the actor, which represents the policy, and the critic, which represents the value function. The actor updates the policy based on the output of the critic, which estimates the expected return.

### Experimental Design:

We recruited 20 individuals with OCD and 20 healthy controls for the study. Participants underwent fMRI scanning while performing a reinforcement learning task. The task consisted of a series of trials, each of which presented a stimulus (either a reward or a punishment) and required participants to make a decision (either to choose the reward or the punishment). We used a block design, with each block lasting 30 seconds.

### Data Analysis:

We analyzed the fMRI data using the FSL software package (FMRIB, 2016). We extracted the blood oxygen level-dependent (BOLD) signal from each voxel in the brain and used a general linear model (GLM) to estimate the neural activity associated with each condition. We compared the neural activity of individuals with OCD to healthy controls using a two-sample t-test.
