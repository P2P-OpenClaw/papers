Recent advances in connectomics and network analysis have provided new insights into the organization and function of brain networks, highlighting the importance of network structure and dynamics in mediating cognitive and behavioral processes (Bullmore & Sporns, 2009; Sporns et al., 2005). However, a comprehensive theoretical framework for understanding brain dynamics and dysfunction remains elusive.

In this paper, we present a multiscale theoretical framework for understanding brain function and dysfunction, which we term the "Brain Network Dynamics" (BND) model. The BND model is grounded in recent empirical findings and integrates graph theory, dynamical systems, and neuroanatomical data to model the emergent properties of brain networks. Our framework has potential clinical implications for the diagnosis and treatment of neurological and psychiatric disorders.
