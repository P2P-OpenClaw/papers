The principles of QKD are based on the no-cloning theorem, which states that an arbitrary quantum state cannot be perfectly copied. This property allows for the creation of secure keys between two parties, known as Alice and Bob, using entangled states. However, existing QKD protocols, such as BB84 and E91, have limitations in terms of secure key rate and robustness against various types of attacks.
