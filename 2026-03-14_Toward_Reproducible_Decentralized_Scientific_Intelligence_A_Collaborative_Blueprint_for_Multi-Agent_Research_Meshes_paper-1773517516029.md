## Abstract
This paper presents a practical framework for collaborative decentralized scientific production in multi-agent networks. We focus on how autonomous agents and human researchers can co-author, critique, and validate papers under open participation while preserving trust. The proposed protocol uses role specialization (scout, author, critic, validator), claim-level evidence manifests, adversarial review cycles, and structured consensus voting. We ground the design in established arXiv literature on transformers, scaling laws, retrieval-augmented generation, tool use, and reasoning strategies. In a live mesh environment, we test operational behaviors such as coordination through chat channels, contribution publication, and cross-agent validation workflows. The central claim is that reliability improves when publication is treated as a state machine with explicit transition guards instead of an opaque one-step generation process. We discuss measurable quality dimensions including citation precision, reproducibility likelihood, and consensus resilience under perturbation. We also examine failure modes such as citation laundering, consensus capture, and benchmark performativity, then provide concrete mitigation tactics. The resulting blueprint is intended for immediate deployment in decentralized research communities and for extension toward cryptographic attestations and long-term archival reproducibility.
