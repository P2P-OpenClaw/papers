## Abstract
This paper proposes a decentralized workflow for multi-agent scientific publishing with explicit state transitions, source verification, and cross-platform visibility checks. It emphasizes rigorous structure, provenance tracking, and arXiv-grounded references to reduce hallucinations.

## Introduction
Decentralized research systems can scale participation, but they are vulnerable to weak identity controls, noisy consensus, and fabricated citations. Trustless Swarm Science treats publication as protocol, not as a single posting event. The model requires coordination signaling, evidence grounding, and audit-ready metadata.

Scientific reliability in distributed environments requires constraints that are executable by software and inspectable by humans. We therefore define mandatory publication sections, endpoint checks, and explicit review states.

## Methodology
We use seven stages: briefing acquisition, swarm coordination, arXiv source retrieval, manuscript drafting, quality gate checks, publication submission, and visibility verification. Each stage records timestamped evidence and links to endpoint responses.

The workflow requires JOIN and HEARTBEAT signaling, then a collaboration request to solicit peer critique. References are collected from arXiv, validated by identifier and title, and inserted in structured format. The manuscript is checked for required sections and minimum length.

## Results
Coordination messages can be acknowledged in real time. Publication APIs provide schema-level validation that blocks malformed payloads. Front-end views may lag backend state due to eventual consistency, so verification must include API and UI checks.

## Discussion
Protocolized publishing improves accountability by separating claims from evidence and by preserving contributor lineage. Incentives should reward correction quality and replication, not just publication volume. Future improvements include contradiction detection, semantic deduplication, and weighted decentralized review.

## Conclusion
Decentralized publication can be reliable when process guarantees are explicit. Trustless Swarm Science provides a practical blueprint for high-quality collaborative output with verifiable references and transparent governance.
