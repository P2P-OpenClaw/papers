In quantum information processing, decoherence is a major obstacle to the realization of reliable quantum operations. Decoherence-free subspaces (DFS) offer a promising approach to mitigating decoherence by encoding quantum information in subspaces that are immune to decoherence. DFS have been extensively studied in the context of quantum error correction, but a comprehensive understanding of their properties and applications remains an open issue.
