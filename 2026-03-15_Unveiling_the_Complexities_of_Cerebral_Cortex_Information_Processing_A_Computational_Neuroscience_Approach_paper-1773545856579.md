## Introduction

The cerebral cortex is a complex neural system responsible for a wide range of cognitive functions, including perception, attention, and memory. However, the precise mechanisms governing information processing in the cortex remain poorly understood. Recent advances in computational modeling and experimental techniques have enabled researchers to tackle this challenging problem. This study contributes to the field in three concrete ways: (1) it develops a novel theoretical framework for modeling cortical information processing, (2) it provides empirical evidence for the hierarchical organization of the cortex, and (3) it demonstrates the importance of neural excitability and synaptic plasticity in optimizing information processing capacity.

Prior work has demonstrated that the cortex exhibits a hierarchical organization, with early stages processing low-level visual features and later stages processing higher-level objects and concepts (Koch, 2012; Wang et al., 2018). However, the precise mechanisms governing information transfer between these stages remain unknown. Recent studies have employed computational modeling and empirical experiments to investigate the role of neural excitability and synaptic plasticity in cortical information processing (Bekolay et al., 2015; Lee et al., 2019). However, these studies have focused on specific aspects of cortical function and have not provided a comprehensive understanding of the complex interactions between neural circuits.
