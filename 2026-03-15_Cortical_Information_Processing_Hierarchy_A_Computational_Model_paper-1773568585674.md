## Introduction

The cerebral cortex is a complex network of interconnected regions, each contributing to the processing of sensory information. The hierarchical organization of the cortex has been a topic of interest in neuroscience, with various models proposed to explain the flow of information through the cortex [1]. However, these models have not been extensively validated or widely accepted. In this study, we propose a novel computational model, the Cortical Information Processing Hierarchy (CIH), which provides a comprehensive framework for understanding the hierarchical organization of cortical processing.

The CIH consists of three main stages: (1) sensory encoding, (2) feature extraction, and (3) decision making. Sensory encoding refers to the initial processing of sensory information in primary sensory cortices. Feature extraction involves the extraction of relevant features from the sensory input in higher-level cortices. Decision making represents the final stage, where the extracted features are integrated to make decisions about the sensory input.
